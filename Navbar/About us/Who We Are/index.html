Questions and Answers (FAQ) on Decode Age

All live cells contain the essential coenzyme NAD+ (nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide), which is necessary for DNA repair, energy consumption, and cellular aging.

Nicotinamide Mononucleotide, or NMN, is a precursor of NAD+ that the body uses to increase NAD+ levels.

NAD+ is preceded by NMN. Our bodies use NMN to convert it into NAD+ when it is eaten, which helps to increase the naturally lowering levels of NAD+.

Age-related decreases in NAD+ levels are caused by oxidative stress, DNA damage, and lifestyle choices. Numerous biological processes may be impacted by this reduction.

Increasing NAD+ levels with NMN may help manage weight, promote a healthier metabolism, reduce aging, enhance memory and cognition, and fight fatigue.
