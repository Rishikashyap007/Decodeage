Our bodies need calcium alpha-ketoglutarate, or Ca-AKG, for the synthesis of amino acids, the synthesis of energy, and the methylation of DNA. It is well known for its possible ability to promote general health and slow down biological aging.

Our bodies naturally produce less alpha-ketoglutarate (AKG) as we become older. Restoring these levels through the use of a Ca-AKG supplement may reduce biological aging, increase energy, and improve general health.

Ca-AKG has been associated with maintaining the mitochondria's function—the cells' power plants—and with triggering the AMPK pathway, which aids in preserving the equilibrium of energy within the cell. Its potential function in lifespan and healthy aging may be enhanced by these actions.

Ca-AKG may promote general health, which may obliquely aid in the treatment of chronic illnesses. For instance, it can help with metabolic processes, which may be advantageous for diseases like diabetes. To completely comprehend its impacts, more research is necessary.
