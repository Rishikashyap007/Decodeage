What is calcium alpha-ketoglutarate, or Ca-AKG, and how does it function?

This article explores the history, chemistry, biology, and possible health advantages of Ca-AKG and clarifies its importance.

Let's Discuss Calcium Alpha-Ketoglutarate, or Ca-AKG.

Alpha-ketoglutarate (AKG), a chemical essential to metabolic processes, and calcium combine to form Calcium Alpha-Ketoglutarate (Ca-AKG), a substance that is becoming more and more popular in scientific and health-conscious circles.

The Past and the Present

The voyage of Calcium The discovery of AKG, a crucial part of the Krebs cycle that Hans Adolf Krebs first postulated in 1937, marked the beginning of AKG. For all aerobic creatures to generate energy, this cycle is essential. The subsequent combination of calcium and AKG, which has been recognised for its essential role in the human body since antiquity, was a major breakthrough in nutritional research.

Biology and Chemistry

Our cells constantly create alpha-ketoglutarate, which is essential to the Krebs cycle and helps produce ATP, the main source of energy for our cells. Alkaline earth metal calcium is necessary for blood clotting, muscle contraction, nerve transmission, and the formation of bones and teeth. The combination of calcium AKG and essential metabolic pathways may enhance the bioavailability of both components when taken as a supplement due to its chelated state.

Ca-AKG: What is it?

Calcium Alpha Ketoglutarate, or Ca-AKG, is a substance that is essential to the body's cellular functions. It consists of calcium and alpha-ketoglutaric acid, a crucial step in the Krebs cycle, commonly referred to as the citric acid cycle, which is how our cells produce energy.

AKG's Function in Autophagy

Autophagy is one of the main processes in which Ca-AKG is involved. The body uses autophagy, to put it simply, to recycle and eliminate damaged or malfunctioning cells and cell components. The general health and functionality of our cells, tissues, and organs depend on this process.

The general health and functionality of our cells, tissues, and organs depend on this process. Our body's capacity for autophagy declines with age. Age-related disorders and faster ageing are associated with this reduction. Ca-AKG can be quite helpful in supporting our body's autophagy process, which is why it is so important.

The Relationship Between Ca-AKG and Lifespan

An extensive body of research has demonstrated a direct correlation between long life and Ca-AKG supplementation. According to one such study that was published in Cell Metabolism, mice that received Ca-AKG supplementation lived 12% longer than mice that did not receive it. Studies on fruit flies and roundworms have also produced encouraging findings, with a 15% increase in fruit fly lifespans and a 50% increase in roundworm lifespans. The important longevity gain is thought to be caused by Ca-AKG's involvement in inducing autophagy and its effects on cellular health and function in general.

Ca-AKG's Function in Cellular Energy Balance

Apart from its function in autophagy, Ca-AKG also triggers the activation of AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK), an enzyme that is essential for preserving the equilibrium of energy within cells. This protein ensures that our cells have enough energy to do their many tasks by monitoring and controlling the energy levels in our cells. Ca-AKG can promote good blood sugar regulation and energy balance, both of which are critical for general health and directly affect ageing, by activating AMPK.

Ca-AKG serves as an antioxidant as well.

The antioxidant qualities of Ca-AKG are still another important advantage. Reactive oxygen species (ROS) and other toxic substances build up under conditions of oxidative stress, which is known to cause cellular damage and ageing. Ca-AKG neutralises ROS and may slow down aging-related processes as an antioxidant. Furthermore, Ca-AKG is a precursor to glutamate, which is necessary for the body to produce glutathione, a powerful antioxidant. Ca-AKG strengthens the body's defences against oxidative damage by promoting the synthesis of this antioxidant.

Ca-AKG Promoting Adult Bone and Muscle Health

Our bone and muscle strength inevitably deteriorates with age. Because it facilitates the synthesis of collagen, an essential protein for these tissues, calcium-AKG can support the maintenance of bone and muscle health. Our muscles, skin, and bones are supported and given structure by collagen, which keeps them robust and healthy.

Fascinating Information and Upcoming Studies

Space Investigation

NASA has investigated the effectiveness of Calcium AKG in maintaining bone density in microgravity, demonstrating its wide range of possible uses.

Anti-Aging and Extended Life

Although early research on anti-aging shows promise, Calcium AKG cannot yet be considered a "fountain of youth."

Can Supplements Contain Ca-AKG?

Supplemental calcium alpha-ketoglutarate (Ca-AKG) has garnered interest due to possible health benefits, particularly regarding energy metabolism and ageing. It's essential to comprehend their safety profile in order to use them wisely. Here's what guidelines and recent studies suggest:

1.Overall Safety:

Research on Ca-AKG has generally demonstrated that it is safe for ingestion by humans, with no notable side effects recorded at dosages up to 4.5 grammes taken over three years.

2.Longevity and Health Research:

Research on health and longevity has mostly focused on animal models, where Ca-AKG has demonstrated encouraging outcomes in terms of increasing lifespan and enhancing HealthSpan. Even while these studies appear promising, it's crucial to remember that there are still few human studies, and additional study is required to completely comprehend the effects on human health.

3. Individual Considerations:

Individual medical problems, current medications, and general health status should all be taken into account while using any supplement. Individuals who are on particular medications or have specific health issues have to speak with a healthcare provider before to beginning any new supplement, including Ca-AKG.

4.Factor of Age:

Because the body produces less AKG as people age, supplementing with calcium-aKG may be more beneficial for older adults, particularly in terms of bone, muscle, and metabolic health.
